The 4/42 Architecture

Why 42 signals?
Because your engine is a system of flows.

Most scanners read a handful of numbers and call it a day. We asked a different question: what if you treated every engine like four interconnected systems, each one telling part of the story?

Throughput Base

"What's flowing in and out right now."
Air, fuel, heat, exhaust — the raw materials your engine consumes and produces every second. 10 signals tracking intake volume, fuel delivery, throttle response, and atmospheric conditions.

Process Rate

"How efficiently is it being converted to work."
Combustion timing, fuel trim corrections, engine load — 8 signals measuring whether your engine is burning fuel efficiently or wasting it.

Flow State

"What's the real-time state right now."
Oxygen sensors, catalyst health, driver behavior scoring — 5 signals that put the numbers in context. Are you cruising efficiently or hammering the throttle?

System Lifecycle

"How long will things last."
Coolant, battery, check engine status, component degradation — 6 signals predicting what's about to fail before your dashboard warns you.

4 primitives × 10+ signals each = 42 governance nodes.
Read 10 times per second. Computed deterministically. Zero AI. Zero guesswork.
It's the difference between a thermometer and a full physical.

FAQ

Questions

Is there a subscription?

Sort of. You make a one-time software purchase (from $9.99) and pay a small monthly service fee ($1.99/mo at Founders pricing, locked for life) for continuous updates, new features, and cloud diagnostics. Cancel anytime — you keep the software. Or choose Own Outright ($249) for lifetime access with zero recurring fees. See pricing →

How much will I actually save?

At $3.50/gallon and 15,000 miles/year, most drivers save $180–$320 annually through fuel coaching alone. The app pays for itself in about 2 weeks.

Do I need a mechanic to install it?

No. Plug the adapter into the OBD-II port under your steering column. Takes 10 seconds. No wiring, no tools, no modifications.

What's the difference between this and a cheap code reader?

A code reader tells you P0420. Lume Scan tells you your catalyst is degrading at 1.2% per month, predicts failure in 6 weeks, and has been coaching you to save $23/month on fuel the entire time.

Is my vehicle data private?

Yes. All data stays on your device. We don't collect, sell, or share your driving data. See our Privacy Policy.

Will the proprietary dongle be required?

No. Lume Scan will always work with any BLE OBD-II adapter. The proprietary dongle unlocks extended diagnostics and vehicle-carried logging, but the core 42-signal engine works with any $15-$30 adapter.

What about adapter returns?

The OBD-II adapter is a standard commodity device purchased separately from Amazon or other retailers. We do not sell, manufacture, or warranty adapters. For adapter returns or exchanges, contact Amazon or the original retailer directly. We guarantee our software and firmware — not third-party hardware.

What makes the diagnostic output trustworthy?

Every scan result is cryptographically hashed (SHA-256) and anchored to the Trust Layer Ledger — a public, immutable verification chain. Your diagnostic data is mathematically provable, tamper-proof, and independently verifiable. No other consumer diagnostic tool offers this.
